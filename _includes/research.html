Using nearby galaxies to better understand more distant galaxies

Using resolved stellar populations to characterize biases in spectral fitting


I am interested in the intersection of resolved star studies of galaxies and integrated light studies of galaxies. For nearby galaxies, we can study both perspectives simultaneously: HST imaging reveals the resolved stellar populations and SDSS-IV MaNGA IFUs provide the spatially resolved spectroscopy. My thesis work focuses on characterizing the translation of integrated light into meaningful astrophysical quantities of galaxies, especially on sub-kpc scales where stochastic processes can cause widely-used approximations to break down.


Including nebular emission for more complete models of galaxy light


I'm also interested in properly accounting for nebular line and continuum emission in SPS models, and have implemented a nebular model in FSPS. I'm interested in better connecting star formation rate indicators with the associated stellar and nebular flux, including the influence of bursty star formation histories and massive star evolution.

Other research interests


From the resolved stellar population side of things, I use evolved stars in M31 to constrain stellar evolution models as a part of PHAT. I am also really excited about 2D spectroscopic surveys of galaxies, and have worked on both survey science and instrumentation as a part of MaNGA.
