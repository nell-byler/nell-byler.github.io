Nebular Emission in FSPS

I have implemented a gas model within FSPS that self-consistently computes the contribution of nebular line and continuum emission for stellar populations using the photoionization code Cloudy. The model is described in Byler+2017.


You can use FSPS or python-FSPS to compute the nebular contribution for complex stellar populations. The nebular model is self-consistent for stellar populations generated using MIST, Padova, PARSEC, and BPASS evolutionary tracks.

The UV properties of star-forming galaxies

Light emitted from the first galaxies in the universe has been substantially redshifted: photons that are emitted in the ultraviolet (UV) will be observable in the infrared. Studies of these first galaxies with next-generation facilities (e.g., the James Webb Space Telescope ) will thus rely on UV diagnostics. While diagnostics in the optical are well-characterized, UV diagnostics are not well established. I published a suite of UV emission and absorption line diagnostics in Byler+2018, and I am currently working on comparing different galactic properties derived with UV and optical diagnostics.

LIER-like emission from post-AGB stars

In Byler+2019, I explored the ionizing properties and colors of hot, evolved stars. I found that post-AGB stars naturally reproduce LIER-like emission line ratios and equivalent widths, and that a small population of blue horizontal brainch stars can explain the UV-excess seen in some early-type galaxies. Using resolved stellar populations to characterize biases in spectral fitting

I am interested in the intersection of resolved star studies of galaxies and integrated light studies of galaxies. For nearby galaxies, we can study both perspectives simultaneously: HST imaging reveals the resolved stellar populations and SDSS-IV MaNGA IFUs provide the spatially resolved spectroscopy. My research focuses on characterizing the translation of integrated light into meaningful astrophysical quantities of galaxies, especially on sub-kpc scales where stochastic processes can cause widely-used approximations to break down.



Other research interests


From the resolved stellar population side of things, I use evolved stars in M31 to constrain stellar evolution models as a part of PHAT. I am also really excited about 2D spectroscopic surveys of galaxies, and have worked on both survey science and instrumentation as a part of MaNGA.
